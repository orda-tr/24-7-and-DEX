Executive Summary

24/7 Traditional Asset Trading — Hedged via DEX Perpetuals

Product Overview
  • Launch 24/7 trading in traditional assets: Oil, Gold, Silver, NASDAQ, S&P 500, major equities
  • Hedge ~100% of client exposure using perpetual futures on DEX
  • Hedging logic: Client goes long → we hedge long on DEX (same direction). Net cost ≈ 0
  • Risk materializes when DEX execution diverges (slippage, ADL, oracle failures)
Key Risk Considerations
  • Slippage on Massive Closures: Large simultaneous client exits force hedge closure at unfavorable prices — cost scales non-linearly with NOP size
  • Fast Market / Leverage Mismatch: If hedge leverage exceeds client avg leverage, hedge liquidates first → unhedged window until client SL fires
  • ADL Risk: Insurance fund depletes → hedge forcibly closed — fully unhedged
  • Oracle / Smart Contract Risk: $3.1B lost in DeFi H1 2025 alone
Scenario Analysis Summary
  • Scenario 1 — Slippage on Mass Closures: 20% NOP closure → ~$5.6K (NASDAQ normal) to $1.65M (Gold stress ×5)
  • Scenario 2 — Fast Market Weekend: Client leverage > hedge leverage → HC lower by ~95% vs. client leverage ≤ hedge leverage | Under stress (×5): Gold/Oil/Silver at $25M–$50M NOP → full margin wipeout ($5M–$10M)
  • Scenario 3 — ADL: Insurance fund depletion → forced hedge closure → cost up to $6.5M on $50M NOP
  • Scenario 4 — Oracle Failure: Wrongful liquidation → margin loss 50–100% → cost up to $10M on $50M NOP
Recommendation
  • Start with conservative exposure caps and phased rollout
  • Keep hedge leverage below client average leverage to ensure client SL fires first
  • Hedge leverage must be carefully balanced: too high → liquidation risk in fast markets; too low → excess capital locked on Hyperliquid exposed to counterparty risk
  • Cap total client NOP per asset based on DEX liquidity profile — illiquid assets (Silver, Gold) require stricter NOP limits
  • Real-time monitoring of insurance fund health, oracle integrity, slippage thresholds
  • Pre-defined circuit breakers and manual intervention protocols

Regulatory Risk

Risk of regulators classifying the DEX protocol as an unlicensed financial service

Key Risk Drivers
DriverDescription
Legal ClassificationRegulators may interpret DEX use as engaging with unauthorized exchanges or derivatives platforms
Jurisdictional GapsDEXs operate without a legal domicile — unclear which jurisdiction's rules apply to eToro's hedging activity
MiCA & Evolving RulesEU MiCA framework (2024–2025 rollout) may impose new obligations on firms using DeFi infrastructure for commercial purposes
Governance AccountabilityUnlike centralized exchanges, DEXs lack governance and recourse. Regulators may view this as inconsistent with operational risk standards
Price Feed DependencyOn weekends, prices may rely solely on the DEX oracle — single point of failure that could trigger erroneous SL/TP executions
Business ContinuityIf the DEX experiences downtime on weekends, eToro has no fallback. Current BCP frameworks do not address this scenario
⚠️ Potential Consequences
• Regulatory fines or enforcement actions
• License suspension or restriction
• Mandatory cessation of DEX-linked activity
• Reputational damage with institutional counterparties
✅ Mitigating Measures
• Obtain formal legal opinions
• Maintain clear audit trail of all DEX-related activity
• Monitor MiCA / CFTC / FCA guidance continuously
Risk Level:High Regulatory classification risk is material and cannot be fully mitigated through structural measures alone. Requires ongoing legal oversight.

Anti-Money Laundering exposure from permissionless, pseudonymous DEX activity

Key AML Risk Drivers
DriverDescription
Permissionless AccessAny wallet interacts without KYC — illicit actors can participate freely
Counterparty OpacityPseudonymous addresses — no ability to identify underlying users or assess source of funds
Sanctions ExposureMaterial risk of interacting with sanctioned entities or illicit funds (fraud, hacks, darknet)
Fund ComminglingLiquidity pools mix legitimate and illicit assets — isolation of specific exposure is structurally impossible
No AML ControlsDEXs lack KYC/KYB, sanctions screening, transaction monitoring, or SAR filing capabilities
Mitigating Measures
• Legal opinion — eToro does not hold client funds; trades execute via smart contracts directly against the protocol
• Blockchain analytics (e.g., Chainalysis) — screen wallets, monitor flows
• Internal policies — defined DEX risk appetite, escalation procedures
• Limitation: commingling significantly reduces screening effectiveness
Residual Risk
Counterparty verification: impossible
Source of funds tracing: structurally limited
Sanctions screening: partial at best
Regulatory enforcement exposure: active
AML Risk Level:High — Inherent DEX trading cannot be fully de-risked from an AML perspective. Risk must be explicitly acknowledged and accepted at senior level.

Potential for traders to manipulate asset prices or trading activity on the DEX

Manipulation Vectors
TypeMechanismImpact
Oracle ManipulationAttacker moves spot price on low-liquidity DEX → oracle reports false price → wrongful liquidation of eToro hedgeCritical
Spoofing / Wash TradingLarge fake orders inflate apparent liquidity → eToro hedge enters at worse price, liquidity vanishes on exitHigh
Sandwich AttackMEV bots front-run and back-run eToro's large hedge orders → forced to execute at worst available priceHigh
Liquidity WithdrawalLPs remove capital just before eToro needs to exit hedge → slippage spikes dramatically at worst momentMedium
Pump & Dump on PerpCoordinated price spike forces DEX insurance fund depletion → triggers ADL, forcibly closes eToro's hedgeCritical
Why DEXs Are Particularly Vulnerable
• No surveillance — no exchange-level manipulation detection
• Transparent mempool — large pending orders visible to MEV bots
• Thin order books — small capital can meaningfully move prices
• No recourse — on-chain transactions are irreversible
✅ Mitigating Measures
• Use TWAP execution to reduce MEV exposure
• Multi-venue hedging to avoid single manipulation point
• Real-time oracle health monitoring
• Position size limits relative to DEX OI
• Circuit breakers on abnormal spread / slippage events
Risk Level:High eToro's large hedge sizes relative to DEX OI make it a prime target for manipulation. Execution strategy is critical.
